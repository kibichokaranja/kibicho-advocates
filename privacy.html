1. Who We Are & Data Controller Details

Kibicho & Kibicho Advocates is a licensed law firm registered with the Law Society of Kenya (LSK), headquartered at Runda, Kiambu Road, Nairobi, Kenya.

For the purposes of the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 and the GDPR, Kibicho & Kibicho Advocates is the Data Controller of your personal data.

Data Protection Officer: privacy@kibichoadvocates.co.ke
Postal Address: P.O. Box 00000, Runda, Nairobi, Kenya

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Contact information: Full name, email address, phone number, postal address
  • Case information: Legal matter descriptions, case documents, evidence files, case type, urgency level
  • Identity documents: Where required for legal representation — copies of ID, passport
  • Financial information: Payment details processed via secure third-party payment providers
  • Lawyer application data: Bar membership number, CV, credentials, practice areas, professional biography
  • Communications: Messages sent via our AI chat, contact forms, email, or WhatsApp
  • Newsletter subscriptions: Email address for legal updates

2.2 Data Collected Automatically

  • Usage data: Pages visited, time on site, browser type, device type, IP address
  • Cookies: Session cookies, preference cookies, analytics cookies (see Section 8)
  • Log data: Server logs, error reports, performance data

2.3 Sensitive Personal Data

In the course of legal representation, we may process special category data (health information, criminal records, financial details, biometric data for DNA referrals). This is processed only with your explicit consent or where necessary for legal proceedings, in accordance with Section 30 of the Kenya DPA 2019 and Article 9 GDPR.

3. How We Use Your Data

  • Providing legal services, case management, and advocate matching
  • Operating and improving our AI legal assistant platform
  • Processing DNA testing referrals through accredited laboratory partners
  • Communicating case updates, legal developments, and firm news
  • Processing applications from lawyers seeking to join our network
  • Sending legal update newsletters (where you have subscribed)
  • Complying with our legal and regulatory obligations as a law firm
  • Preventing fraud, money laundering, and other illegal activity
  • Improving our platform, services, and user experience
  • Responding to enquiries and providing customer support

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data on the following lawful bases:

  • Contract performance: Processing necessary to provide legal services you have requested
  • Legitimate interests: Improving our services, fraud prevention, network security
  • Legal obligation: Compliance with anti-money laundering laws, court orders, regulatory requirements
  • Consent: Newsletter subscriptions, cookies, processing of special category data — you may withdraw consent at any time
  • Vital interests: Where necessary to protect life (emergency legal situations)

5. Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell your personal data to third parties. We may share data in the following circumstances:

  • Verified advocates in our network: Lawyers assigned to represent you receive your case information necessary to provide representation
  • DNA laboratory partners: For DNA testing referrals, your referral information is shared with accredited laboratory partners under strict data processing agreements
  • Service providers: Hosting providers, payment processors, email delivery services — all bound by data processing agreements
  • Legal and regulatory authorities: Where required by law, court order, or regulatory obligation
  • Professional advisors: Auditors, insurers, and legal advisors of the firm, under confidentiality obligations

All third-party processors are contractually bound to process your data only on our instructions and in compliance with applicable data protection law.

6. Data Retention

  • Active client files: Retained for the duration of your legal matter plus 7 years (as required by the Advocates (Practice) Rules and LSK guidelines)
  • Enquiry data (non-clients): 12 months from last contact
  • Newsletter subscribers: Until you unsubscribe
  • Lawyer application data: 12 months for unsuccessful applications; ongoing for approved network members
  • Financial records: 7 years for tax and accounting compliance
  • Website analytics: 26 months (anonymised after 12 months)

7. Your Rights

Under the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 and GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention obligations)
  • Right to restrict processing:< Ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Right to data portability: Receive your data in a portable format
  • Right to object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time
  • Right to lodge a complaint: With the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) in Kenya, or your local data protection authority

To exercise any of these rights, contact: privacy@kibichoadvocates.co.ke. We will respond within 30 days.
